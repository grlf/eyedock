Plaquenil Risk Calculators

Disclaimer: This tool is designed to help eye care professionals better understand the risk the of retinotoxicity from hydroxychloroquine. Any concerns regarding dosage should be discussed with the prescribing physician.

Discussion
A risk factor for Plaquenil (hydroxychloroquine) retinotoxicity is a daily dose that exceeds 6.5 mg/kg (or 3.0 mg/kg for chloroquine). The tool on the left simply calculates this threshold based on body weight.

As hydroxychloroquine is not absorbed by adipose tissue it is possible to overestimate the safe daily dose in overweight individuals. For this reason I have provided the option of using the ideal body weight for the safe dosage calculation. This is determined using equations that take into account gender and height.

It's important to understand that the daily dose is only one risk factor for Plaquenil retinotoxicity. Other risk factors include:

  • Obesity (as described above)
  • Duration of use (cumulative dose)
  • Renal or hepatic functional impairment. Compromised kidney and/or liver function can lead to increased accumulation of hydroxychloroquine in the tissues.
  • Age over 60 years.
  • Preexisting retinal disease

While a high daily dose is a risk factor for retinotoxicity, cumulative dose appears to be a greater risk factor. The risk of a toxicity sharply increases after 5 years, with majority of cases of retinotoxicity occurring in patients that have had a culmulative dose exceeding 1000g of hydroxychloriquine (Plaquenil).

This level is reached in about 7 years with the most common daily dose of Plaquenil, 400 mg/day (200 bid).
